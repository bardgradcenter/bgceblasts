Swedish Wooden Toys
On view September 18, 2015–January 17, 2016

Swedish Wooden Toys is organized by Bard Graduate Center. It has been jointly curated by Dr. Susan Weber and Dr. Amy F. Ogata, professor of art history at the University of Southern California and former professor at Bard Graduate Center.

Swedish Wooden Toys is generously supported by Proventus AB and Gregory Soros with additional funding from the Barbro Osher Pro Suecia Foundation.

Special thanks to the Consulate General of Sweden in New York and The American-Scandinavian Foundation.

Revisions—Zen for Film
On view September 18, 2015–January 10, 2016

Curated by Hanna Hölling, 2013-2015 Andrew W. Visiting Professor, Cultures of Conservation, in collaboration with the participating students of Bard Graduate Center.

Revisions—Zen for Film was developed during a two-year Andrew W. Mellon “Cultures of Conservation” Fellowship at Bard Graduate Center.

Bard Graduate Center is a graduate research institute in New York City. Our Gallery exhibitions and publications, MA and PhD programs, and research initiatives explore new ways of thinking about decorative arts, design history, and material culture. Founded in 1993, the Center is an academic unit of Bard College. Visit bgc.bard.edu for accompanying program information, and store.bgc.bard.edu to purchase publications.
