Artek and the Aaltos: Creating a Modern World
April 22, 2016–September 25, 2016

Artek and the Aaltos is organized by Bard Graduate Center. It has been jointly curated by Nina Stritzler-Levine, Director, Bard Graduate Center Gallery, and Juhani Pallasmaa, architect, educator and author.

Artek and the Aaltos: Creating a Modern World has been generously supported by Artek. Additional funding provided by the National Endowment for the Arts, and Graham Foundation for Advanced Studies in the Fine Arts. Special thanks to the Consulate General of Finland in New York. Additional support for the catalogue has been provided by Furthermore: a program of the J. M. Kaplan Fund.

Frontier Shores: Collection, Entanglement, and the Manufacture of Identity in Oceania
April 22, 2016–January 10, 2016

Frontier Shores is curated by Shawn Rowlands, Bard Graduate Center-AMNH Fellow in Museum Anthropology.

Bard Graduate Center Gallery organizes pioneering exhibitions on decorative arts, design history, and material culture with leading scholars, curators, and institutions worldwide. We provide opportunities for faculty and students to gain experience in exhibition making. Our projects and publications break down traditional barriers between academic and curatorial forms of inquiry.

Bard Graduate Center Gallery publications are on sale at the exhibition and online at store.bgc.bard.edu.

To learn about public programs, tours, and general information, please visit our website at bgc.bard.edu.
