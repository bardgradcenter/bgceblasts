Visions of the future usually tell us more about when they were made than about the time period they seek to depict. In this talk, Howard Besser will examine how 20th-century films and television shows have depicted computers and related information systems. In a presentation liberally illustrated with clips, he will show how these views of the future, which range from wildly inaccurate to prescient, and are often very funny,provide insights into the social and cultural preoccupations of the period in which when they were made.
